Relevent Courses and Extracurriculars

At Washington and Lee University, I have immersed myself in a diverse range of courses that have not only fueled my passion for learning but also challenged me to expand my skills. This academic journey has been instrumental in preparing me for a rewarding career in Software Engineering.

Software Development

Throughout this course, I gained a comprehensive understanding of software development methodologies, programming languages, and best practices. I actively participated in real-world projects, honing my skills in software design, coding, debugging, version control, and software testing. This hands-on experience has equipped me to build scalable and maintainable software applications.

Theories of Computation

Exploring the theoretical foundations of algorithms and computation models fascinated me in this course. I delved into formal languages, automata theory, and computability theory, gaining insights into the mathematical principles that govern the limits and capabilities of algorithms. This deep understanding of algorithmic complexity has greatly influenced my approach to problem-solving.

Discrete Mathematics

Understanding the mathematical structures and concepts that are countable or distinct, has been instrumental in my knowledge of algorithm design and data structures. The topics covered, including logic, set theory, combinatorics, and graph theory, have provided the logical and mathematical foundations necessary for navigating the theoretical aspects of computation in software engineering.

Computer Organization

In this course, I delved into the architecture and structure of computer systems. I studied processor design, memory hierarchy, input/output systems, and even engaged in assembly language programming. This foundational knowledge has been invaluable, bridging the gap between high-level software and low-level hardware in my understanding of computer systems.

Linear Algebra

With its focus on vector spaces, matrices, eigenvalues, and linear transformations, this course has been a cornerstone in my academic journey. This course is crucial for applications in graphics rendering, machine learning, and data analysis within software engineering. The insights gained here have enhanced my problem-solving abilities.

Multivariable Calculus

Extending the principles of calculus to functions of several variables was a challenging yet rewarding experience. This course covered partial derivatives, multiple integrals, and vector calculus. I now possess the mathematical tools necessary for modeling and analyzing complex systems encountered in software engineering.
